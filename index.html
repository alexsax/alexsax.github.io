Alexander (Sasha) Sax

Researcher @ FAIR

Meta AI

About me:

I am currently a post-doc on the Embodied AI team at FAIR (Meta AI research), where I work on representation learning for Embodied AI systems. Applications of this are in systems that need to make sense of the physical world: robots, AR/VR, and 3D scene understanding.
My research focuses on developing ways to train visuomotor representations that support sample-efficient training of downstream tasks, the ability to generalize to novel situations, and to adapt to unforeseen changes in the environment or robot.
One approach that I'm particularly excited about is using consistency constraints as a source for self-supervision + lifelong calibration (e.g. cross-calibration of sensory modalities/across viewpoints/over time).
Before this I was at UC Berkeley, where I was advised by Jitendra Malik and Amir Zamir (at EPFL). Before coming to Berkeley, I got my BS in Math and MS in CS, where I was advised by Silvio Savarese.
